How to Apply for QA Roles

Finding a job in QA is a job in itself. Here is a systematic approach to getting your application in front of the right people and converting it into interview calls.

1 Build Your Skills First

Before you start applying, make sure you have the basics covered. Employers look for:

  • Core testing knowledge (test cases, bug reporting, test planning)
  • Familiarity with at least one test management tool (Jira, TestRail)
  • Basic understanding of Agile methodology
  • For automation roles: basic scripting knowledge (Python, Java, JavaScript)

2 Optimise Your LinkedIn Profile

Recruiters actively search LinkedIn for candidates. Make sure your profile is complete:

  • Professional headshot and a compelling headline (e.g., "QA Engineer | Manual & Automation | Selenium | Jira")
  • Detailed work experience with achievements and keywords
  • Skills section with relevant tools and technologies endorsed
  • Turn on "Open to Work" and set your preferences
  • Post about your learning journey — it increases your visibility

3 Where to Find QA Jobs

  • LinkedIn Jobs — Set up job alerts for "QA Engineer", "Software Tester", "SDET"
  • Company career pages — Target companies directly, especially startups
  • Job boards — Indeed, Glassdoor, AngelList (startups)
  • Nepal-specific — MeroJob, JobsNepal, Kumarijob
  • Networking — Attend tech meetups, QA conferences, and online communities
  • Referrals — Ask your network if they know of any openings

4 Apply Strategically, Not Just Broadly

Quality over quantity — but also volume matters in early-career job searching.

  • Tailor each application to the specific job description.
  • Write a short, personalised cover letter (3 paragraphs max).
  • Apply to internships even if you have some experience — it gets your foot in the door.
  • Track your applications in a spreadsheet: company, role, date applied, status.
  • Follow up with a polite email 1 week after applying if you haven't heard back.

5 Don't Get Discouraged

Rejection is normal and expected — even experienced engineers face it. Here's how to stay motivated:

  • Set a daily goal: apply to 3–5 relevant roles per day.
  • Ask for feedback when you don't get the job.
  • Continue learning while you search — it keeps your skills fresh and your confidence up.
  • Celebrate every interview, even the ones you don't pass — each one is practice.
