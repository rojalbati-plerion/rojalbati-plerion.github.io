Prepare for Interview

Getting the interview call is only half the battle. Here are 9 key steps to walk in prepared and confident.

1. Research the Company and Position 🔍

Understand the organisation and the specific role to customise your answers and demonstrate genuine interest. Know the product, the tech stack, recent news, and the company's mission statement.

2. Practice Common Interview Questions 📚

Prepare responses for standard queries like introductions ("Tell me about yourself"), motivation for joining, and examples of overcoming challenges. Practice out loud — not just in your head.

3. Review Your CV ✍️

Bring copies if the interview is in-person. Be ready to discuss every item on your CV — especially the tools, projects, and achievements you listed. Never put something on your CV you can't talk about.

4. Dress Appropriately 👕

Wear professional, neutral clothing. For virtual interviews, ensure good audio and video quality, a suitable background, stable internet connection, and a charged device with a mobile backup.

5. Plan to Arrive on Time ⏰

Arrive early to settle in and mentally prepare. For virtual interviews, join 2–3 minutes early to test your connection. Being late is one of the easiest ways to lose the interview before it starts.

6. Follow the Interviewer's Lead 🤝

Wait for seating instructions before sitting (in-person). Let the interviewer set the tone and pace. Listen carefully before answering — don't rush your responses.

7. Be Honest If You Don't Know an Answer 🙅‍♂️💯

It is completely acceptable to say "I don't know" — and express your willingness to learn. Interviewers respect honesty far more than a confident wrong answer. Never try to bluff your way through a technical question.

8. Follow Up on Unanswered Questions 📩

Contact the interviewer afterward if there were questions you couldn't answer in the moment. Focus discussions on career growth, company direction, and mission rather than immediately asking about salary.

9. Show Appreciation 🙏

Thank the interviewer at the end and express genuine enthusiasm for the position. Send a short follow-up thank-you email within 24 hours — very few candidates do this, and it makes a strong impression.
