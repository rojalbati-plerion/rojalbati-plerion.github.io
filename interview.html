Prepare for the Interview

Getting the interview call is only half the battle. Here are 9 key steps to walk in prepared, confident, and memorable.

1. Research the Company and Role 🔍

Understand the organisation and the specific position before you walk in. Know the product, the tech stack, recent company news, and the mission statement. Customise your answers to show genuine interest — interviewers can tell the difference immediately.

2. Practice Common Interview Questions 📚

Prepare responses for standard queries like "Tell me about yourself", "Why do you want to work here?", and "Describe a challenge you overcame." Practice out loud — not just in your head. The difference in delivery is significant.

3. Review Your CV ✍️

Be ready to discuss every item on your CV in detail — especially tools, projects, and achievements you've listed. Bring printed copies for in-person interviews. Never put something on your CV you can't speak confidently about.

4. Dress Appropriately 👕

Wear professional, neutral clothing that reflects the company culture. For virtual interviews: ensure strong audio and video quality, a clean background, stable internet, and a fully charged device with your phone as backup.

5. Arrive (or Log In) Early ⏰

Arrive 10 minutes early to settle, breathe, and mentally prepare. For virtual interviews, join 2–3 minutes before start time to test your connection. Being late — for any reason — is one of the easiest ways to lose the role before it starts.

6. Follow the Interviewer's Lead 🤝

In person: wait for seating instructions before sitting. Let the interviewer set the tone and pace. Listen carefully before responding — pausing to think is far better than rushing to fill silence.

7. Be Honest If You Don't Know the Answer 🙅‍♂️

Saying "I don't know, but here's how I'd find out" is far more impressive than bluffing. Interviewers respect intellectual honesty and a growth mindset. Never try to wing a technical question — experienced interviewers will know immediately.

8. Follow Up After the Interview 📩

If there were questions you couldn't answer in the moment, reach out afterward with a well-researched response. Keep follow-up conversations focused on career growth, company direction, and mission — save salary discussions for the offer stage.

9. Show Genuine Appreciation 🙏

Thank the interviewer at the end and express authentic enthusiasm for the role. Send a short, personalised thank-you email within 24 hours. Very few candidates do this — it makes a strong, lasting impression.

Congratulations  👏

You've completed the full beginner's guide. Now go get that role — you've earned it.
