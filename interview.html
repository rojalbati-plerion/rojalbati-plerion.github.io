Ace Your QA Interview

Getting the interview call is only half the battle. Here is how to walk in prepared, confident, and ready to impress.

Types of QA Interview Rounds

Most QA interviews follow this structure:

  • HR Screening — Background, salary expectations, cultural fit, notice period.
  • Technical Round 1 — Core QA concepts, test case writing, bug reporting, SDLC/STLC.
  • Technical Round 2 — Deeper testing scenarios, tools, possibly a live test case exercise.
  • Automation Round (if applicable) — Live coding, framework design, code review.
  • Final Round — Manager or director, behavioural questions, team fit.

Common QA Interview Questions

  • What is the difference between severity and priority?
  • What is regression testing? When do you perform it?
  • How do you write a test case for a login page?
  • What is exploratory testing? Give an example.
  • What is the difference between black-box and white-box testing?
  • How do you decide what to automate?
  • How do you handle a disagreement with a developer about a bug?
  • What is your process when you find a critical bug just before release?
  • Tell me about a time you found a bug that others missed.

How to Answer Behavioural Questions

Use the STAR method for any behavioural question:

  • Situation — Set the scene. Where were you? What was the context?
  • Task — What was your responsibility or challenge?
  • Action — What specific actions did you take?
  • Result — What was the outcome? Use numbers if possible.

Example: "Tell me about a time you found a critical bug before release." → Describe the project, your responsibility, how you found it and escalated it, and the impact it had on the release.

Questions to Ask Your Interviewer

Always prepare 3–5 questions to ask at the end. This shows genuine interest and helps you evaluate the company:

  • What does the QA process look like on your team?
  • What is the ratio of manual to automated testing?
  • How does QA collaborate with developers and product managers?
  • What does success look like in the first 90 days?
  • What are the biggest challenges the QA team is currently facing?

Before the Interview — Checklist

  • Research the company: product, tech stack, recent news.
  • Review the job description and prepare examples for each key requirement.
  • Prepare your "Tell me about yourself" answer (2 minutes, focused on QA).
  • Test your internet, camera, and microphone if it's a video interview.
  • Have a glass of water and a notepad nearby.
  • Send a thank-you email within 24 hours of the interview.
