Frequently Asked Questions

Would you be able to effectively determine the quality of food if you had no knowledge about it? Of course not! Similarly, coding knowledge helps you better understand software quality. However, it is not a strict requirement to start your QA journey.

You can begin as a manual tester and learn coding along the way. I personally learned Java and Selenium after starting as a manual tester. What matters most is your willingness to learn — those who embrace coding tend to make greater contributions and earn higher compensation over time.

Here are five recommendations to improve your chances:

  1. Develop your core skills and build a solid foundation in QA terminology and processes.
  2. Create a comprehensive, well-structured CV that highlights your relevant skills and experience.
  3. Keep your LinkedIn profile up to date with your learnings, certifications, and projects.
  4. Research the tools and technologies used by experienced QA engineers and add them to your skillset.
  5. Apply broadly, including for internships — every application is a learning opportunity.

If you have creativity, a user-focused mindset, teamwork skills, and strong organizational abilities, you are well-suited to excel in QA. These are the traits that define great testers.

Even if you don't feel naturally talented, hard work and dedication can absolutely make up for it. The QA field welcomes individuals from diverse backgrounds — what matters is your passion for quality and your commitment to continuous learning.
