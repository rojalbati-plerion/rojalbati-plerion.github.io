CV Structure

  1. Personal Information — Full name, address (city/country), phone with country code, professional email (firstname.lastname@domain.com), GitHub link, LinkedIn link.
  2. Summary / Objective — Show your interest in the company, role, and industry. Research the company mission and tech stack. Keep it to 3–4 lines.
  3. Skills — Match the job description. Include only relevant skills; highlight unique competencies aligned with the role.
  4. Professional Experience — Focus on accomplishments and value added. Example: "Automated smoke tests using Cypress, reducing regression time by 50% in an Agile environment."
  5. Education — Most recent degree first. Include GPA/percentage if strong. Recent graduates should place Education above Skills.
  6. Certifications / Achievements — Include only relevant certifications (e.g. ISTQB for QA roles).
  7. Hobbies / Interests — Keep it authentic and personal.
  8. References — Simply write "Available upon request."

10 Best Practices

1

Tool: Use Google Docs for easy sharing and real-time editing.

2

Research: Check LinkedIn profiles of QA engineers at your target companies to spot required skills early.

3

ATS: Mirror keywords from the job description. Aim for at least 7 out of 10 required skills. Use TopResume for a free review.

4

Length: Keep it to 1–2 pages maximum.

5

Quality: Use Grammarly, maintain consistent formatting, stick to minimal colors, and verify every link.

6

Referrals: A company referral dramatically increases your chances — aim for one when possible.

7

Naming: Save your file as "CV [Full Name] – [Position Title]".

8

Format: Submit as PDF to preserve layout and enable browser preview.

9

Timing: Apply on Monday mornings before office hours for maximum visibility.

10

Omit: Never include your date of birth or salary expectations.
